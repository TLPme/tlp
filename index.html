Statement on AI

I don't compete with AI. I direct it.

I use AI to accelerate research, test structural variations, surface counterpoints, and pressure-test arguments. It expands range and speed. It does not make decisions.

What clients hire me for is discernment — knowing what to keep, what to cut, what to question, and what not to publish. Voice, risk calibration, ethical framing, tonal control, and audience judgment remain human responsibilities. Those are mine.

AI can generate language. It cannot assume accountability. It cannot understand political nuance, brand risk, cultural subtext, or long-term reputational cost. I can.

The result is not "AI writing." It is informed, strategically engineered communication shaped by human judgment.
